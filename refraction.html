Refraction

Certain materials, such as glass and water, allow light to pass through it, but slower than it would in air or vacuum. The amount that is slowed down in a material is called the index of refraction (IOR).

This slowing down causes light to bend, or refract, at the surface of the material. The angle at which light bends can be found using Snell's law.


Snell's Law

When passing from one material to another, the angle at which light refracts (θ r) can be found with this equation:

n1sin(θi) = n2sin(θr)
